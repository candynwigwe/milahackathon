Key Findings & Actionable Insights
✅ Perfect Label–Risk Alignment
Risk and Label are 100% correlated: every high-risk conversation is label=1, every low-risk is label=0. These two columns are redundant — use one as the ground truth target.
⚠️ Class Near-Balance but Slight Imbalance
Label 0 (55.3%) vs Label 1 (44.7%) — a ~10% imbalance. Not severe, but monitor precision/recall for label=1 in model evaluation. Consider stratified splits.
🔥 Suicide Category = Highest Risk Rate
64.3% of Suicide conversations are label=1 (9/14). Romantic/Sexual is 100% (1/1). These high-risk categories are underrepresented — only 15 combined rows.
📊 No Outliers in Turns or Text Length
IQR analysis found zero outliers in both Turns and Text Length. The distributions are platykurtic (flat) — conversations are spread evenly across the range, not concentrated.
📉 Turns & Text: Not Predictive Alone
Statistical tests show no significant difference in turns or text length between label 0 and 1 (p > 0.84). Conversation length alone is not a risk signal.
🌐 Language Mix is Realistic
67% English, 18% French, 15% Mixed. The label=1 rate is consistent across all three languages — no language bias detected. Good for multilingual model robustness.
📦 Small Dataset — 94 Rows
This is a seed/validation set only. Several categories have very few examples (Hate-Speech: 1, Romantic/Sexual: 1, Grief/Loss: 1). Statistical power is limited — treat chi² results cautiously.
🎯 Category Distribution is Skewed
Social Relationships (29) and Isolation (26) dominate — 58% of all rows. Bullying, Grief, Hate-Speech, and Romantic categories have ≤2 rows each. Severe underrepresentation.
